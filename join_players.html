    Why should a player join the OPA?

    It is unfortunate but true that the international online gambling industry is largely unregulated, and that means two things:

    The player needs to do his or her homework to avoid involvement with a rogue casino and certain types of lower quality casino operators can and do indulge in bad casino practise to the player's disadvantage.

    The Online Players' Association gives players the strength of unity and community, with free membership
    • a private message board that enables the exchange of vital information with other honest and experienced gamblers, and industry specialists.
    • an informative newsletter based on input from OPA researchers and other volunteer experts serving on the OPA Players' Advisory Board.
    • unbiased and expertly assembled lists of "Approved" and "Not Recommended" operations
    • a free, influential dispute resolution service that has already recovered many hundreds of thousands of dollars for cheated players. Someone to fight your corner with skill and determination when you have a genuine case.
    To obtain the experienced OPA dispute resolution service free of charge you must have been a member of the OPA at the time the events complained about took place. A small $50 fee is charged upfront if you were not a member at the time but subsequently become one.
